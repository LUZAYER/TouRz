Ladakh

Destination Details

Ladakh is known for its natural beauty, including its mountains, lakes, and valleys, as well as its rich culture and traditions:
Mountains
Ladakh is surrounded by snow-capped peaks, some over 7,000 meters high. Visitors can enjoy trekking through the mountains, such as on the Markha Valley Trek, Stok Kangri Trek, or Chadar Trek.

Lakes
Ladakh has many beautiful lakes, including Pangong Lake, the world's highest saltwater lake. The lake's blue water contrasts with the surrounding arid mountains. Other lakes include Tso Moriri and Tso Kar.

Valleys
Ladakh has deep valleys and wide open valleys. The Nubra Valley in the north of Leh and the Zanskar Valley in the south are popular destinations.

Culture
Ladakh has a rich culture and traditions, with a mix of Tibetan and Indian architecture. Buddhist monasteries are located on hilltops and in mountain gorges.

Motorable passes
Ladakh has some of the highest motorable passes in the world, including the Kahrdungla pass, Changla pass, and Tanglangla Pass.

Skies
Ladakh is known for its crystal clear skies.
